### [1:00 – 2:30] Deep-Dive Case Study: RouteGenius

#### **(Camera: Transition to screen-share of the RouteGenius interface or architecture diagram)**

"To give you a clear look at how I manage engineering complexity, let's look at **RouteGenius**—a web traffic distribution engine I built to solve a critical monetization issue for a digital media company.

The client needed to understand which incoming traffic segments and monetization paths were driving revenue in real-time, but they lacked an attribution mechanism. I engineered an infrastructure-level routing engine capable of dynamically distributing web traffic across multiple destination flows based on custom percentage weights.

The architectural challenge lay in the real-time feedback loop. Each traffic path fed data directly into a multi-agent AI workflow built in n8n via concurrent webhooks. These agents monitored behavioral signals, session depth, and monetization events, processing data instantly to adjust routing configurations.

To make this successful, I solved three core engineering hurdles: ensuring high-throughput webhook reliability under heavy concurrent loads, structuring agent prompts to guarantee deterministic analytics with zero correlation hallucinations, and building a responsive Next.js front-end that enabled stakeholders to seamlessly adjust routing parameters without touching the database.

The outcome was a fully automated pipeline that eliminated ad-spend waste, isolated high-performing conversion funnels, and provided a scalable framework for live monetization testing."
