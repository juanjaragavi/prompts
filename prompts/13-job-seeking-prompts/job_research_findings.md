# Job Research Findings: DataAnnotation & BJAK

## 1. "AI/ML Developer - AI Trainer" at DataAnnotation

### Direct Application & Job Pages
- **Landing / Info Page**: [https://www.dataannotation.tech/coding](https://www.dataannotation.tech/coding)
- **Direct Sign Up / Application Page**: [https://www.dataannotation.tech/signup](https://www.dataannotation.tech/signup)
- **Indeed Posting (Example - Pleasanton, CA)**: [https://www.indeed.com/viewjob?jk=a81aeda5d73436dd](https://www.indeed.com/viewjob?jk=a81aeda5d73436dd)

### Compensation
- **Salary / Pay Rate**: Starting at **$50.00 to $100.00+ USD per hour** on an hourly contract basis, depending on project complexity and prompt difficulty. Highly technical coding and machine learning prompts scale to $100+/hr. Offers performance-based bonus rates.
- **Payment Method**: Weekly payouts processed strictly via **PayPal** (including currency conversion from USD).

### Job Description & Responsibilities
DataAnnotation is seeking AI/ML Developers (Software Engineers/Coders) to evaluate, write, and train generative AI coding models. This is a 100% remote, contract-based, completely flexible on-demand role with no standups, sprint commitments, or on-call rotations. Tasks are available 24/7.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Design and solve diverse, challenging coding problems to train AI systems.
  - Craft precise, complex engineering prompts to test model limits and expose incorrect algorithms, unsafe memory access, or flawed system design decisions.
  - Write clear, high-quality, production-grade code snippets and detailed explanations.
  - Evaluate AI-generated code for accuracy, performance, safety, and clarity. Inspect and catch off-by-one errors, race conditions, N+1 queries, transactional isolation levels, bundle optimization, security vulnerabilities, cryptographic misuse, and injection vectors.
  - Author complete, production-quality correct implementations to serve as training signal for the next generation of AI models.
  - Provide structured reviews similar to senior-level code reviews.

### Requirements & Qualifications
- **Programming Languages**: Proficiency in at least one major language/framework, including **Python, JavaScript, TypeScript, React, Go, Java, Kotlin, Swift, C, C#, or C++**.
- **Location Constraints**: Strictly limited to applicants currently living in the **United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Ireland, Australia, or New Zealand**.
- **Education**: A bachelor's degree (completed or in progress) is preferred.
- **Experience**: Previous experience as a Software Developer, Software Engineer, Programmer, or Coder is preferred.
- **Skills**: Fluency in English (native/bilingual), excellent technical writing and grammar skills, strong logical reasoning, and a passion for deep analytical work.

### Specific Submission Requirements & Application Process
- **Resume/CV**: **NOT required**. No resume submission is needed.
- **Evaluation/Screening**: Register an account on the platform and complete a short onboarding **Engineering Assessment (~60 minutes)**. It tests the applicant's ability to spot bugs, reason about complexity, and write clean, idiomatic code. 
- **Approval**: Upon passing, applicants receive email confirmation and immediately gain access to 24/7 paid projects.

## 2. "Applied AI Engineer" at BJAK

### Direct Application & Job Pages

- **Direct Application Page (Example - India Remote)**: [https://jobs.ashbyhq.com/bjakcareer/d6c58233-5692-4820-8b86-52dade2e7e19](https://jobs.ashbyhq.com/bjakcareer/d6c58233-5692-4820-8b86-52dade2e7e19)

### Compensation
- **Europe/Switzerland Remote**: **$95,727.50 to $144,000 USD per year** (as listed on Remote OK).
- **India Remote**: **INR 1,800,000 to 3,000,000 (18 - 30 LPA)** annually.
- **Malaysia (On-site/Hybrid)**: **MYR 60,000 to 90,000** annually.
- **Benefits**: Remote-first setups with flexible working hours, home office allowances, and dedicated professional development budgets.

### Job Description & Responsibilities
Applied AI Engineers at BJAK turn raw model capabilities into real-world product workflows and behaviors for BJAK's A1 Neobank and financial super-app. This is a hands-on, builder-oriented engineering role rather than a pure research position.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Build AI agents, workflows, tools, and automations that solve real customer, product, and operational challenges.
  - Implement LLMs, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), tool calling, guardrails, persistent context, and long-running workflows.
  - Create AI-assisted experiences for support automation, digital onboarding, KYC, risk reviews, CRM, and document handling.
  - Own system performance end-to-end (model -> system -> user experience), optimizing for latency, cost, and accuracy constraints.
  - Debug issues across the full stack (model behavior, orchestration, infrastructure, UX) and develop lightweight evaluation frameworks to measure production quality.

### Tech Stack & Qualifications
- **Core Tech Stack**: **Python, PyTorch / JAX, LLMs (OpenAI-style APIs, LLaMA, Qwen, etc.), Inference Serving (e.g., vLLM), and Vector DBs** (as well as TypeScript/JavaScript in some departments).
- **Experience**:
  - Hands-on engineering foundation; experience building AI agents, LLM applications, copilots, or RAG-enabled tools.
  - Strong coding skills and ability to write clean, maintainable, production-ready code.
  - Fintech, banking, risk, fraud, operations automation, or support automation experience is a significant advantage.
  - Solid technical career highlights demonstrating personal contribution to shipping/scaling serious products.
- **Language**: Excellent English communication skills.

### Specific Submission Requirements & Application Process
- **Resume/CV**: Required. Can be uploaded via Ashby's portal (PDF, docx, etc.), which has an autofill feature.
- **Profile Fields**: Standard contact info (Name, Email, Phone, Current location) plus LinkedIn URL and GitHub URL.
- **Consent and Visas**: Preliminary questions on work visa status and legal consent to process personal recruitment data.
- **Required Screening Questions**:
  1. *Which best describes your experience with machine learning systems?*
     - A. I have built and owned ML systems deployed in production and used by real users
     - B. I have contributed to ML systems that were deployed in production
     - C. I have trained or experimented with models, but not deployed to production
     - D. My experience is primarily coursework, research, or benchmarks
  2. *Which statement best reflects what you enjoy most?*
     - A. Shipping ML systems, observing real-world behavior, and iterating under constraints
     - B. A balance of applied research and production work
     - C. Research, papers, benchmarks, and experimental exploration
     - D. Managing people or research agendas rather than building systems
  3. *Have you worked on ML systems where you had to consider at least one of the following? (Select all that apply)*
     - Latency or throughput constraints
     - Compute or cost constraints
     - Model failures or unexpected behavior in production
     - Safety, misuse, or long-term behavior
     - None of the above
- **Interview Process**: Moves very quickly (completed in ~1 week for strong candidates):
  1. Online assessment or practical task.
  2. Technical interview.
  3. CEO / Final round.
