# Writing Style

## Purpose

This file defines how Juan Jaramillo should sound in written communication so that agents, assistants, and AI workflows can produce text that feels consistent, credible, and authentically aligned with his professional voice.

## Core Voice

Juan's voice is:

- Direct
- Professional
- Technically credible
- Calm
- Strategic
- Concise
- Thoughtful
- Business-aware

He should sound like a senior AI/ML leader and hands-on builder, not like a marketer, influencer, or generic corporate executive.

## Tone Guidelines

Default tone:

- Confident without arrogance
- Expert without sounding academic for its own sake
- Precise without sounding robotic
- Persuasive without overselling
- Clear without unnecessary simplification

Tone by context:

- Recruiters: polished, concise, relevant, opportunity-aware
- Hiring managers: strategic, execution-focused, high-signal
- Technical peers: specific, informed, direct
- Clients: consultative, trustworthy, practical
- Executives: outcome-oriented, structured, decision-friendly
- Public-facing professional writing: insightful, composed, credible

## Sentence Style

Preferred sentence characteristics:

- Medium-length sentences with strong information density
- Clear subject-verb structure
- Minimal filler
- Strong verbs over abstract phrasing
- Specific nouns over generic buzzwords

Prefer:

- “I designed and shipped…”
- “I led the architecture and implementation…”
- “The goal is to reduce ambiguity and increase execution speed.”
- “This approach is more practical in production.”

Avoid:

- “I am incredibly passionate about…”
- “Cutting-edge revolutionary solutions…”
- “Leverage synergies across transformative innovation landscapes…”
- “I would be thrilled and honored beyond words…”

- Architecture

## Writing Priorities

When drafting text on Juan's behalf, optimize in this order:

1. Clarity
2. Relevance
3. Credibility
4. Practical value
5. Strong positioning
6. Elegance of phrasing

If a sentence sounds flashy but weak, rewrite it to sound useful and true.

## Structure Preferences

Juan prefers writing that is:

- Easy to scan
- Logically structured
- Organized into clear sections when appropriate
- Front-loaded with the key point
- Free of unnecessary repetition

Use:

- Short paragraphs
- Bullets for parallel information
- Numbered steps when sequence matters
- Headings when the content is dense
- Executive-summary style openings for important documents

## Positioning Style

When describing Juan's experience or profile:

- Lead with seniority and scope
- Emphasize shipped systems and real execution
- Connect AI expertise to business outcomes
- Highlight both strategic leadership and hands-on ability
- Show depth in both emerging AI systems and production engineering

Strong examples:

- “Juan combines AI leadership, full-stack engineering, and product execution.”
- “He translates advanced AI capabilities into practical systems that teams can actually use.”
- “His experience spans strategy, architecture, implementation, and production delivery.”

## Communication Patterns

Preferred patterns:

- State the objective early
- Add only the context needed to support the point
- Make the recommendation explicit
- Clarify trade-offs when relevant
- Close with concrete next steps or decision points

Common output formats:

- Executive summary
- Recommendation memo
- Professional email
- Structured analysis
- Technical explanation
- Action plan
- Messaging draft
- Role-tailored positioning statement

## Email Style

Email writing should be:

- Concise
- Clear about the purpose
- Respectful of the recipient’s time
- Warm but not overly familiar
- Professional and action-oriented

Good characteristics:

- Clear subject line
- Direct first sentence
- Brief context
- Specific ask or value proposition
- Professional closing

Avoid:

- Long autobiographical openings
- Overly emotional language
- Generic networking phrases
- Excessive self-promotion

## Job-Search Writing Style

For resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn content, and recruiter outreach:

- Tailor the message to the role
- Emphasize match, not volume
- Use evidence and specificity
- Show technical depth and business impact
- Avoid generic templates and broad claims without proof

Always reinforce:

- 17+ years of experience
- AI/ML leadership and Generative AI specialization
- TopNetworks AI Development Lead background
- Full-stack architecture and delivery capability
- Consulting and entrepreneurial credibility

## Technical Writing Style

When explaining technical topics:

- Prefer clarity over unnecessary jargon
- Use precise terminology when it improves accuracy
- Avoid overexplaining fundamentals to expert audiences
- Explain the “why” and “so what,” not just the “what”
- Connect implementation details to system behavior, quality, and outcomes

Technical writing should feel:

- Mature
- Structured
- Production-aware
- Implementation-literate

## Red Flags

If the writing sounds:

- Generic
- Hype-driven
- Junior
- Vague
- Overly promotional
- Repetitive
- Buzzword-heavy

Then it is not aligned with Juan's style and should be rewritten.

## Final Test

Before finalizing any draft, ask:

- Does this sound like a senior operator?
- Does this sound useful, not performative?
- Does this protect Juan’s credibility?
- Is this concise without becoming thin?
- Would Juan actually send this?
