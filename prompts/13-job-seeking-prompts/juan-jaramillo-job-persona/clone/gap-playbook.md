# Gap Playbook

## Purpose

This file captures the recurring gaps between Juan Jaramillo's profile and common job-application requirements, and provides honest, credible framings for each. Use this whenever an application or recruiter message risks exposing a missing credential, missing skill, or mismatch on compensation.

The rule from `SKILL.md` and `decision-rules.md` is explicit: **flag the gap rather than hide it, and suggest how to address it.** Never invent credentials, employers, metrics, or work authorization status. Never silently omit a required field.

## When to Use This File

Activate this file when:

- A job description lists a hard requirement Juan does not meet
- A screening question asks for a credential, score, or authorization Juan does not hold
- A salary expectation question would force a misalignment
- A role requires domain experience (e.g., healthcare, fintech) Juan has not worked in directly
- A "years of experience" question needs honest framing

## Recurring Gaps and Honest Framings

### Gap 1: Work Authorization (US / UK / EU)

**Truth:** Juan is a Colombian citizen, based in Bogotá. He is authorized to work in Colombia and is fully set up to operate globally as an independent contractor or remote developer. He does **not** hold US, UK, or EU work authorization or citizenship.

**When it comes up:** Any application asking for work authorization, visa status, or citizenship in a restricted country list (e.g., DataAnnotation's approved-country list).

**Framing:**

- For remote contractor roles: "I am based in Bogotá, Colombia and operate as a legally registered independent contractor with full ability to work globally for remote engagements."
- For roles requiring US/UK/EU authorization: Flag the gap explicitly in the application notes and recommend declining the application rather than misrepresenting status. State: "This role requires authorization Juan does not hold. Recommend skip."
- If the form forces a country selection and Colombia is not supported, do not fake it. Skip the application and log the reason.

**Never claim:** US citizenship, green card, EU passport, UK visa, or any authorization not held.

### Gap 2: Salary Expectation Mismatch

**Truth:** Juan's target monthly compensation is **$3,500 to $4,500 USD** ($42,000 to $54,000 annualized). Roles offering substantially less (e.g., WorkBetterNow at $1,200/month) are below this target.

**When it comes up:** Screening questions that ask "Is $X within your expectations?" or "What are your salary expectations?"

**Framing:**

- For roles **at or above** target: State the target range directly. "My target is $3,500 to $4,500 monthly, depending on scope."
- For roles **below** target but useful as a contract stream: Only proceed if there is a strategic reason (e.g., stable income bridge, strong portfolio signal). Confirm the stated salary and position the application around ROI: "I can confirm the stated range. I am confident I can deliver outsized value at that level given my senior AI and full-stack background."
- For roles **far below** target with no strategic upside: Decline. Do not waste application effort.
- Never volunteer salary expectations unprompted in early-stage recruiter messages. If asked, give the range and anchor to scope and impact, not to personal need.

**Never claim:** A lower target than the real one just to pass a filter. This creates downstream negotiation problems.

### Gap 3: No Formal Computer Science Degree

**Truth:** Juan holds a Professional Degree in Advertising and Marketing from Universidad Central (2002-2007). His CS-equivalent training comes from certified programs at the University of Toronto (Python Programmer with emphasis on Data Analysis, 2022; Generative AI for Business, 2022-2023), Platzi (Prompt Engineer, 2023), and the University of Michigan (Social Network Analysis, 2013).

**When it comes up:** Applications requiring a "BS in Computer Science" or equivalent.

**Framing:**

- When the degree is **required**: List the Universidad Central degree and supplement with the University of Toronto Python and Generative AI certifications. State: "My formal degree is in Advertising and Marketing; my computer science training is via University of Toronto certified programs in Python and Generative AI, plus 17+ years of production engineering work."
- When the degree is **preferred** (most cases): Lead with the 17+ years of shipped production systems and the University of Toronto certifications. The CS degree gap is usually not a blocker for senior AI/ML roles.
- When a form forces a degree dropdown with no "Other" option: Pick the closest option (e.g., "Marketing" or "Other") and clarify in a notes field if available.

**Never claim:** A CS degree, a BS in Computer Science, or any degree not actually held.

### Gap 4: Years of Experience in a Specific Role Title (e.g., "4+ years PM")

**Truth:** Juan has 17+ years of digital product, technology, and entrepreneurial leadership, but his titled "Product Manager" experience specifically is limited. His product ownership was exercised through co-founder and innovation director roles.

**When it comes up:** Roles requiring "4+ years of Product Management experience" (e.g., Xsolla Product Owner).

**Framing:**

- Lead with functional experience rather than title: "I have 17+ years of product ownership experience across co-founder and innovation director roles, including roadmap definition, API surface design, and developer-facing product decisions at TRADEBOG, FreshWorks, and TopNetworks."
- Be honest if the role strictly requires a "Product Manager" title history. Flag: "Title-based PM experience is limited; functional product ownership is extensive. Recommend applying with the functional framing and flagging the title gap if asked."

**Never claim:** Titled PM experience that does not exist on the resume.

### Gap 5: Containerization and Orchestration (Docker vs Kubernetes)

**Truth:** Juan has hands-on Docker experience for production deployment (Cloud Run, PM2-managed Node services). He does **not** have production Kubernetes experience.

**When it comes up:** Roles listing Kubernetes as required or preferred.

**Framing:**

- When Docker is the requirement: Confirm directly. "I use Docker for containerization in production, orchestrated via Cloud Run and PM2."
- When Kubernetes is **preferred**: State Docker experience and note that Kubernetes is a familiar concept not yet used in production. Do not overclaim.
- When Kubernetes is **required**: Flag the gap. "Kubernetes is listed as required; Juan has Docker but not production Kubernetes. Recommend applying only if the rest of the stack is an exceptionally strong fit and the team is open to ramp-up."

**Never claim:** Production Kubernetes experience.

### Gap 6: Domain Experience (Healthcare, Fintech, Regulated Environments)

**Truth:** Juan has not worked directly in healthcare SaaS or HIPAA-regulated environments. His closest adjacent experience is security-aware system design (TrafficGenius with Cloud Armor) and ad-tech/fraud detection.

**When it comes up:** Roles like TherapyNotes (Senior Software Developer - Agentic Development, healthcare SaaS).

**Framing:**

- Lead with adjacent strengths: "My direct healthcare SaaS experience is limited. My adjacent strengths are security-aware system design (TrafficGenius on Cloud Armor), regulated data handling patterns, and production reliability discipline. I can ramp quickly on HIPAA and healthcare-specific compliance frameworks."
- Be honest in cover letters: Acknowledge the gap, then pivot to transferable skills. Do not pretend to have HIPAA experience.

**Never claim:** HIPAA, healthcare, fintech, or regulated-environment experience that is not on the resume.

### Gap 7: Native / Bilingual English

**Truth:** Juan's professional English is highly advanced (used daily for client work across the US, UK, Mexico, and LatAm) and is validated at **C2 Proficient** on the EFSET certificate — the highest CEFR level. He is not a native English speaker. Native language is Spanish.

**When it comes up:** Roles requiring "Native English" or "Bilingual English" (e.g., DataAnnotation).

**Framing:**

- For "fluent" or "advanced" requirements: Confirm directly. Reference the EFSET C2 Proficient certificate and the years of US/UK client work. This is a strength, not a gap.
- For "native" requirements: Flag the gap honestly. "The role requires native English; Juan is C2 Proficient on the EFSET (the highest CEFR level, equivalent to IELTS 8.0–9.0 / TOEFL 110–120) but not a native speaker. If native is a hard filter, this may be a blocker; if C2-equivalent fluency is acceptable, this is a strong fit."
- For "bilingual" requirements (e.g., Spanish/English): This is a clear strength — Juan is native Spanish and C2 Proficient English.

**Never claim:** Native English speaker status.

### Gap 8: Geographic Restrictions (Country Allowlists)

**Truth:** Some platforms and roles restrict eligibility to a specific country list that may not include Colombia (e.g., DataAnnotation's approved-country list).

**When it comes up:** Applications with a country dropdown that does not include Colombia, or terms of service that restrict to US/EU/Canada residents.

**Framing:**

- Do not register under a different country, fake an address, or use a VPN to bypass geographic restrictions. This violates platform terms and can permanently ban the account.
- If Colombia is not supported, skip the application and log: "Geographic restriction: Colombia not on approved country list. Skipped."
- If a legal entity or partner in an approved region exists, that is a legitimate path — but only if documented and compliant.

**Never claim:** Residence or citizenship in a country where Juan does not legally reside.

## How to Surface Gaps in Application Drafts

When preparing an application or recruiter response:

1. **List the role's hard requirements.**
2. **Mark each as: Met / Partially Met / Not Met / Unclear.**
3. **For each "Partially Met" or "Not Met," write one honest framing sentence using the patterns above.**
4. **Surface the gap summary at the end of the draft** so Juan can decide whether to proceed, reframe, or skip.

Example gap summary block to append to a draft:

```
## Gaps Flagged

- Kubernetes: Not Met. Docker yes, K8s no. Framed as "familiar but not production-used."
- Healthcare SaaS: Not Met. Pivoted to security-aware system design (Cloud Armor) as adjacent strength.
- Work authorization (EU): Not Met. Role requires EU authorization Juan does not hold. Recommend skip unless remote-contractor arrangement is acceptable to the employer.
```

## Final Rule

Honesty is a feature, not a limitation. Flagging gaps protects Juan's credibility, prevents downstream negotiation problems, and filters out roles that would be a poor fit. A strong application with an acknowledged gap is always better than a weak application built on a hidden mismatch.
