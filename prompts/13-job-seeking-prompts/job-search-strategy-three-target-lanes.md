## Positioning Strategy

Juan's differentiator is combining two rarely-paired skill sets: deep GenAI/LLM engineering (PEFT, RLHF, Vertex AI, LangGraph, CrewAI) with 15+ years of hands-on WordPress/CMS and front-end work. Most AI-hiring candidates lack the CMS/legacy-web depth, and most WordPress developers lack LLM fine-tuning credentials — this dual profile should be the headline, not buried in a chronological résumé.

- Lead with "AI-Native Full-Stack Architect" positioning rather than generic "AI Development Lead," since "AI-native" and "ai-tools" are now recognized vibe-tags in job listings.[5]
- Reframe the TopNetworks SaaS ecosystem (EmailGenius, TrafficGenius, RouteGenius, Social Media Genius) explicitly as "vibe-coded" AI-assisted builds — naming the workflow (AI IDE, Cursor-style agent-driven development, rapid prototyping) since employers now screen for this term directly.[2][6]
- Keep three résumé/portfolio variants tailored to each track: (1) AI/LLM Engineering, (2) Vibe Coding / AI-Native Product Builder, (3) Full-Stack CMS + React Developer.

## Track-Specific Action Plan

**AI-based development (target: $200k–$253k roles)**

- Emphasize fine-tuning (PEFT/RLHF), Vertex AI/Gemini, and agent frameworks (LangChain, LangGraph, CrewAI) — these map directly to the highest-paying skill categories (fine-tuning $253k avg, agents $232k avg).[3]
- Build one public case study demonstrating an agentic workflow (multi-agent orchestration via CrewAI/LangGraph) with measurable business outcomes, since "agents" is the single largest demand category (2,766 openings).[3]
- Target titles: AI Engineering Lead, LLM/GenAI Engineer, AI Solutions Architect.

**Vibe coding (target: $137k–$200k, remote-first)**

- Update LinkedIn/portfolio language to explicitly say "vibe coding," "AI-native," and "AI IDE workflows" — these are the exact terms recruiters are searching, and Python, TypeScript, and prompt engineering are the top requested skills in this segment.[5]
- Package a portfolio piece showing spec-first, iterative AI-assisted build process (the emerging best practice: write spec → build incrementally → review architecture) to demonstrate professional rigor beyond "prompt and pray".[6]
- Watch for the newly emerging "Vibe Coding Specialist" title, and target companies like Replit, Webflow, GitLab, and Stripe that actively post in this category.[7][8]

**CMS + React/Next.js development (target: $80k–$158k, freelance or hybrid)**

- Position the WordPress background (FreshWorks, La Quinta P Digital Agency) as legacy-modernization expertise — migrating WordPress sites to headless CMS + Next.js/Astro architectures is a high-value niche combining old and new stacks.
- Highlight Next.js 15–16, Astro 5, React 19, and Tailwind v4 work from TopNetworks as direct proof of modern front-end fluency layered on CMS fundamentals.
- Target agencies and SMB SaaS companies needing both marketing-site CMS work and app-level React development — this is where the 336 React+WordPress listings concentrate.[4]
