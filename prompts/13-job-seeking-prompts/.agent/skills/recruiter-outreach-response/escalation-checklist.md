# Escalation Checklist: Recruiter Outreach

Mandatory pre-send verification for any drafted reply to a recruiter
or HR contact via LinkedIn InMail or WhatsApp. Run through this list
before sending; if any item fails, do NOT send automatically -- flag
the draft for Juan's manual review instead.

## Pre-Send Verification Checklist
1. Fact-check: Does the reply contradict any fact in the
   source-of-truth files (resume, CV, cover letter, portfolio)?
2. Scheduling: Does it commit Juan to a specific date/time without
   confirming real availability first?
3. Confidentiality: Does it disclose confidential client/employer
   data (e.g., TopNetworks internal metrics, prior salary figures,
   client names under NDA)?
4. Impersonation check: Is impersonating Juan in first person
   appropriate for this message, per the rules in SKILL.md?
5. Placeholders: Are all bracketed placeholders ([Name], [Role],
   etc.) filled in with real values?
6. Attachment note: Is the attachment line included only on the
   first substantive reply in the thread (not repeated needlessly)?

## Do-Not-Send Conditions (Always Escalate to Juan)
- Sender identity is unclear or looks like spam/phishing (generic
  greeting, suspicious links, too-good-to-be-true offer).
- Recruiter asks for sensitive personal data (national ID numbers,
  bank account details, passport scans) via chat.
- A specific compensation figure is requested that exceeds any
  range Juan has pre-approved.
- The message requires a binding decision (accepting an offer,
  signing a contract, confirming start date) -- always defer to Juan
  directly rather than responding on his behalf.
- The thread involves a legal, visa, or relocation question with
  contractual implications.
- The recruiter references a company or role not mentioned anywhere
  in Juan's CV/resume and asks Juan to confirm undocumented details.

## Escalation Action
When any Do-Not-Send condition is met:
1. Do not send the drafted reply.
2. Present the draft to Juan with a short note explaining which
   condition was triggered.
3. Wait for explicit approval or edits from Juan before sending.
