## [COMPLEX AI PROJECT — RouteGenius — 1:00–2:30]

> "Now, let me walk you through one of the most technically complex AI projects I've built: **RouteGenius**."

> "The problem was this: a digital media company needed to understand which traffic sources, landing pages, and monetization strategies were actually generating revenue — but their current setup had no way to test multiple variants simultaneously or attribute outcomes intelligently. They were flying blind."

> "My approach was to build a **web traffic distribution engine** — a system that receives incoming traffic and routes it across multiple destination URLs, each assigned a specific percentage weight. Think of it as intelligent A/B routing at the infrastructure level."

> "The real complexity came from what happened _after_ routing. Each traffic segment fed into an **AI agent workflow built in n8n**, triggered via webhooks. Those agents were responsible for analyzing monetization signals in real time — evaluating click behavior, session depth, and revenue events across each route — and feeding that data back into a decision loop that could adjust traffic weights dynamically."

> "The challenges were significant. First, webhook reliability at scale — I had to design the pipeline to handle concurrency without dropping events. Second, making the agent logic deterministic enough to produce actionable analytics without hallucinating correlations. Third, building a clean UI that let non-technical stakeholders manage route percentages and read performance data without needing to touch the underlying system."

> "The outcome: the company gained full visibility into which traffic routes and monetization patterns were actually performing. They reduced wasted ad spend, identified their highest-converting paths, and had a repeatable framework for testing new monetization strategies — all driven autonomously by the agent layer."
